About Maharashtra Forts

The Pride of Maratha Swarajya

Land of Forts

Maharashtra is home to more than 300 forts, making it one of the richest regions in India in terms of military architecture and historical heritage. These forts were the backbone of the Maratha Empire and played a crucial role in protecting Swarajya.

Built across hills, forests, and the Arabian Sea coast, Maharashtra forts demonstrate exceptional planning, defense strategy, and architectural brilliance.

Major Historical Forts

Raigad Fort

Raigad was the capital of the Maratha Empire and the coronation site of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1674. It symbolized administrative strength and royal power.

Sinhagad Fort

Famous for the Battle of Sinhagad, this fort represents sacrifice and bravery. Tanaji Malusare laid down his life here, leading to the fort’s capture.

Pratapgad Fort

Pratapgad is renowned for the historic encounter between Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan, marking a turning point in Maratha history.

Hill Forts of Sahyadri

Hill forts were strategically built on mountain peaks to monitor enemy movement and protect trade routes.

Rajgad

Former capital known for strong fortifications.

Torna (Prachandagad)

First fort captured by Shivaji Maharaj.

Shivneri

Birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

Lohagad

Popular fort known for trekking and history.

Sea Forts of Maharashtra

Sea forts protected the Konkan coast and displayed the naval strength of the Marathas.

Sindhudurg

Built by Shivaji Maharaj, Sindhudurg was a powerful naval fort protecting the western coastline.

Murud–Janjira

Known for never being conquered, Murud–Janjira reflects advanced military engineering.

Vijaydurg

One of the oldest sea forts, Vijaydurg served as an important naval base of the Maratha Empire.

Purpose of Gadwata Project

The Gadwata Project is developed to preserve Maharashtra’s historical heritage, educate students, and present history using modern web technologies such as HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, and JavaScript.
